As a center under the Department of Geography at UGA, CGR, along with its team of faculty and students, conducts multidisciplinary research to enhance our understanding of the world. We explore and integrate approaches for data acquisition, processing and analysis, employing novel methods to collect, manipulate, visualize, and interact with data. Our tools address critical and contemporary issues in human-environment relationships and have been used to represent natural and built landscapes, including cultural resources.

Project implementations and technology integrations have strong research and teaching/learning components. We involve undergraduate and graduate students from multiple departments at the University of Georgia in hands-on training and discovery. Results are presented to the UGA/Athens community and beyond during classes, demonstrations, presentations and events.

Research Spotlight

The augmented reality sandbox project at the Center for Geospatial Research is part of a four component system, aiming to bring together multiple technologies for cutting edge data acquisition, virtual/augmented reality and geovisualization for Earth Science education and research. These components are being integrated at the Department of Geography-UGA and create a unique 3D Immersion and Geovisualization (3DIG) experience. The 3DIG system and associated technologies promote increased interest in physical geography, geology and STEM related fields.
